⭐ Drewry WCI Rebounds +3% to $2,286 After Three Consecutive Declines — Transpacific Surcharges Drive Recovery

⭐ Featured ⚡ CUL Direct Finance Ops Global
The Drewry World Container Index (WCI) increased 3% to $2,286 per 40ft container in the week of May 7, ending three consecutive weekly declines. The rebound was driven primarily by the Transpacific trade, where Shanghai–Los Angeles rates rose 4% to $2,934/FEU. Shanghai–New York remained stable at $3,562/FEU. The SCFI composite index also rebounded to 1,954.21 on May 8 (up +42.81 from 1,911.40 a week prior). Carriers imposed new Peak Season Surcharges (PSS) and GRIs across key trades ahead of June.
德鲁里世界集装箱运价指数（WCI）在5月7日当周上涨3%至$2,286/40尺，结束了三连跌态势。反弹主要由跨太平洋贸易驱动，上海→洛杉矶运价上涨4%至$2,934/FEU。上海→纽约维稳$3,562/FEU。SCFI综合指数也于5月8日回升至1,954.21（较前一周1,911.40上涨42.81点）。船公司在六月前在主要贸易航线上征收新的旺季附加费（PSS）和GRI。
