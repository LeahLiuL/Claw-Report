⭐ CMA CGM KRIBI Becomes First Western-Controlled Vessel to Transit Hormuz Outbound
⭐ CMA CGM KRIBI成为首艘经霍尔木兹海峡驶出的西方控制船只
⭐ Featured ⚡ CUL Direct Ops Network Legal Middle East / Global
CMA CGM KRIBI became the first Western-operated vessel to successfully transit the Strait of Hormuz outbound since the US/Israel strikes on Iran (Feb 27). This signals a potential partial reopening of the Gulf corridor under Trump's Apr 7 deadline pressure. Iranian authorities granted passage under conditions reportedly tied to cargo nationality declarations.
CMA CGM KRIBI成为自2月27日美国/以色列袭击伊朗以来，首艘成功经霍尔木兹海峡驶出的西方控制船只。这标志着在特朗普4月7日最后期限压力下，海湾走廊可能出现部分重新开放的信号。伊朗当局据报道在货物国籍申报条件下批准通行。
🎯 CUL Impact: If Hormuz partial reopening is confirmed, CUL Middle East services (Jebel Ali, Dammam, Oman calls) could resume normal routing within 2–4 weeks. Monitor closely — current Cape of Good Hope detour costs CUL est. $300–500K extra fuel per Middle East voyage. Confirm with Hapag-Lloyd / OOCL partners on slot availability for Gulf-bound capacity. 若霍尔木兹部分重开得到确认，CUL中东航线（杰贝阿里、达曼、阿曼挂靠）可能在2-4周内恢复正常路由。需密切关注——当前绕好望角每趟中东航次额外燃油成本估计$30-50万。请向赫伯罗特/东方海外合作伙伴确认海湾向舱位可用情况。
