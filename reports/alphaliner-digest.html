⭐ Featured ⚡ CUL Direct Commercial Trade Network Global
The Drewry World Container Index (WCI) turned higher in the week ending May 7, 2026, rising 3% to $2,286 per 40ft container after three consecutive weekly declines. The rebound was driven primarily by higher freight rates on Transpacific and Asia–Europe trade lanes, as carriers implemented General Rate Increases (GRIs) following the earlier announcement wave from Maersk, MSC, and CMA CGM. Drewry's assessment marks a reversal of the downward trend that saw WCI fall to $2,216/FEU by end of April. However, market participants note that oversupply and demand softness remain structural headwinds — the rebound's sustainability will depend on cargo volume recovery and carrier discipline on capacity management through Q2 2026. Meanwhile, the Drewry composite remains approximately 38% below the peak of ~$3,700 reached during the 2021 supply chain crisis. （数据来源：Drewry WCI, 2026-05-07）
Drewry 世界集装箱运价指数（WCI）在截至 2026 年 5 月 7 日的一周扭转跌势，上涨 3% 至每 40 英尺箱 $2,286，结束了连续三周的下行。此前马士基、MSC 和达飞已宣布 GRI 涨价，推动跨太平洋和亚欧航线运价上升，Drewry 评估因此上调。然而，市场参与者指出，运力过剩和需求疲软仍是结构性逆风——此次反弹的可持续性将取决于二季度货量恢复及船公司运力管理纪律。目前 WCI 综合指数仍较 2021 年供应链危机峰值（约 $3,700/箱）低约 38%。 （数据来源：Drewry WCI, 2026-05-07）

Looking ahead, market focus is shifting to the US tariff situation post the 90-day truce window (mid-July 2026) and whether the anticipated peak-season demand materializes in Q3 2026. The overall freight market is described as entering a period of cautious stabilization rather than strong recovery. （数据来源：上海航运交易所 SCFI, 2026-05-08）

Shippers and freight forwarders are increasingly structuring contracts with Suez-resumption contingencies and rate floor clauses.
