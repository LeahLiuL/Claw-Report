SCFI Ends 10-Week Rally — First Weekly Decline in 4.3%, But Rates Still 77% Above April Lows
SCFI结束十连涨 — 首周下跌4.3%，但运价仍较4月低点高77%
The Shanghai Containerized Freight Index (SCFI) fell 4.3% to 3,184.83 on July 10, ending its historic 10-week consecutive rise. All four major trade lanes declined: USWC -6.2% to $6,219/FEU, USEC -2.0% to $8,134/FEU, Europe -2.5% to $3,332/TEU, Mediterranean -3.3% to $4,561/TEU. South America saw the steepest decline at -9.1%. The pullback reflects the digestion of front-loaded cargo ahead of potential tariff changes, with some carriers easing BCO allocation restrictions from "three spot + one contract" to "two spot + one contract". However, analysts view this as a technical correction rather than a trend reversal, with peak season demand still expected to support rates.
