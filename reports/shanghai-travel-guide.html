Iconic Landmarks
The must-see sights that define Shanghai's skyline and soul
🌃
Waterfront

The Bund (外滩)

Shanghai's most famous promenade stretches 1.5 km along the western bank of the Huangpu River. The north bank is lined with 52 magnificent neoclassical and Art Deco buildings from the colonial era, offering a stunning contrast to the futuristic Pudong skyline across the water. Best visited at night when both sides light up spectacularly.

🕐 All day / best at dusk 💰 Free 📍 Huangpu District
🏙️
Modern Icon

Oriental Pearl Tower (东方明珠)

This 468-metre TV tower in Lujiazui has become the ultimate symbol of modern Shanghai. Visitors can ascend to three observation decks — including a glass-floored sphere — for breathtaking 360° views. The base houses the Shanghai History Museum, tracing the city from fishing village to global metropolis.

🕐 9:00 – 22:00 💰 ¥200 / person 📍 Pudong
🗼
Sky-High

Shanghai Tower (上海中心大厦)

At 632 metres, this is China's tallest building and the world's second tallest. The twisted, double-skin glass façade is an architectural marvel. Rush up to the 118th-floor observation deck — "Cloud Nine" — the world's highest at 546 m, for jaw-dropping views on a clear day. The high-speed elevator reaches the top in just 55 seconds.

🕐 10:00 – 22:00 💰 ¥180 / person 📍 Lujiazui, Pudong
🏛️
Heritage

Yu Garden (豫园)

A classical Ming Dynasty garden nestled within the old city. Built in 1559, its 2 hectares pack ornate pavilions, koi ponds, rockeries, and moon gates into a remarkably peaceful retreat. Surrounding the garden, the Yu Garden Bazaar buzzes with snack stalls and souvenir shops — try the famous xiaolongbao (soup dumplings) at the nearby Nanxiang Steamed Bun Restaurant.

🕐 9:00 – 17:30 💰 ¥40 garden entry 📍 Huangpu Old Town
Must-Visit Neighbourhoods

Arts & Nightlife

Tianzifang (田子坊)

A labyrinth of narrow shikumen (stone-gate) alleyways in Luwan converted into a thriving arts-and-crafts hub. Browse independent boutiques, local artist studios, quirky cafés, and international galleries. Atmospheric at any hour, especially lively on weekend evenings.

📍 Luwan District 💰 Free to walk
🏠
Architecture

Xintiandi (新天地)

An upscale open-air complex of restored 1920s shikumen buildings housing designer restaurants, boutiques, and bars. The north block retains authentic architecture; the south is a modern mall. Also home to the Site of the First National Congress of the CPC — historically significant.

📍 Luwan District 💰 Free entry
🌿
Green Escape

French Concession (法租界)

Tree-lined boulevards, art deco mansions, independent coffee shops and bookstores populate this former French enclave. Huaihai Road is its main shopping artery, while quieter back streets like Wukang Road — centred on the iconic Normandie Apartments — are perfect for a slow afternoon stroll.

📍 Xuhui / Jing'an 💰 Free
🔶
Street Life

Nanjing Road (南京路步行街)

One of the world's busiest shopping streets, stretching 5.5 km from the Bund westward. The pedestrian section between Henan Road and Xizang Road is lined with flagship department stores, neon signs, and street performers. A vibrant sensory overload — and completely free to explore.

📍 Huangpu District 💰 Free
🎨
Contemporary Art

M50 Creative Park (M50创意园)

Shanghai's leading contemporary art district housed in repurposed cotton mill warehouses along the Suzhou Creek. Over 100 galleries and studios occupy the complex; many welcome walk-in visitors. The raw industrial aesthetic makes a compelling backdrop for avant-garde exhibitions.

📍 Putuo / Jing'an 💰 Free – varies by gallery
🌆
Elevated Walk

The High Line — Jing'an Sculpture Park

Inspired by New York's High Line, the Jing'an Sculpture Park and adjacent rail-trail greenway offer a car-free route through the heart of the city. Public sculptures, pop-up markets, and waterside cafés make it ideal for a leisurely afternoon. Adjacent to the West Nanjing Road luxury shopping corridor.

📍 Jing'an District 💰 Free
Museums & Culture
Dive deep into art, history, and the city's living heritage

World-Class

Shanghai Museum (上海博物馆)

One of China's finest museums, renowned for its ancient bronzes, ceramics, calligraphy, and jade collections spanning 5,000 years of Chinese civilisation. Located on People's Square; the building itself is designed to resemble a bronze ding vessel. Free entry, but weekends get busy — arrive early.

🕐 Tue–Sun 9:00–17:00 💰 Free
🖼️
Modern Art

Power Station of Art (当代艺术博物馆)

China's first state-run contemporary art museum, housed in a converted 1985 power plant on the waterfront. Its towering chimney is a Huangpu riverside landmark. Hosts blockbuster international exhibitions alongside Chinese contemporary works. A sister venue to the Shanghai Biennale.

🕐 Tue–Sun 11:00–19:00 💰 ¥20–60 (varies)
🌏
World Expo Legacy

China Pavilion / China Art Museum (中华艺术宫)

The iconic red pyramid-shaped China Pavilion from the 2010 World Expo has been repurposed as a massive museum of Chinese art. It now houses the largest collection of modern and contemporary Chinese art in the world, including a stunning digital recreation of Along the River During Qingming Festival.

🕐 Tue–Sun 9:00–17:00 💰 Free (reservation needed)
Shanghai on a Plate
No trip to Shanghai is complete without eating your way through these classics
🥟

Xiaolongbao

Delicate soup dumplings with pork and crab filling — a Shanghai icon. Din Tai Fung and Nanxiang are top picks.

🍜

Scallion Oil Noodles

Deceptively simple: wheat noodles, crispy scallions, soy sauce. A soul-food staple of every Shanghainese household.

🦀

Hairy Crab

Seasonal (Oct–Dec) delicacy from nearby Yangcheng Lake — steamed whole with vinegar ginger dipping sauce. A true autumn ritual.

🍖

Red-Braised Pork Belly

Slow-cooked in soy, rice wine and sugar until meltingly tender. The quintessential Shanghainese home dish — rich and aromatic.

🥐

Shengjianbao

Pan-fried pork buns — crispy on the bottom, fluffy on top, bursting with broth inside. Best at Yang's Fry-Dumpling.

🍡

Tangyuan (Sweet Rice Balls)

Glutinous rice balls filled with black sesame or red bean paste in warm sweet broth. A comforting winter street snack.

When to Go
Shanghai has something special in every season

🌸 Spring (Mar – May) — Best Season

Cherry blossoms at Gucun Park, warm temperatures (10–22°C), the city at its most photogenic. Tourism peaks but still manageable. Perfect for outdoor exploring.

☀️ Summer (Jun – Aug) — Hot & Humid

Typhoon season, temperatures 28–38°C with high humidity. Offset by long opening hours, outdoor concerts, and Jing'an alfresco dining. Indoor AC is everywhere.

🍂 Autumn (Sep – Nov) — Second Best

Hairy crab season, mild weather, golden ginkgo leaves line the French Concession avenues. The Shanghai Grand Prix and international film festival draw big crowds in October.

❄️ Winter (Dec – Feb) — Festive but Cold

Christmas and Chinese New Year celebrations transform the city with lights and markets. Cold (2–10°C) but rarely snowy. Crowds thin out; hotel rates drop. Indoor culture and hotpot restaurants shine.

Practical Tips
Everything you need to know before you go
🚇

Getting Around

Shanghai Metro (20 lines, 508 stations) is the backbone of the city. Buy a Transport Card for tap-in convenience. Didi (Chinese Uber) is cheaper than taxis.

📱

Mobile Payments

Alipay and WeChat Pay are accepted almost everywhere. Tourists can now link foreign Visa/Mastercard to Alipay's international version — set it up before arriving.

🌐

Internet Access

VPN required for Google, Instagram, WhatsApp etc. Download and activate your VPN before entering China. Most hotels provide solid Wi-Fi.

🗣️

Language

Mandarin is the official language; Shanghainese dialect is spoken locally. In tourist areas, basic English is common. Google Translate offline (Chinese pack) is a lifesaver.

✈️

Airports

Two international airports: Pudong (PVG) for long-haul and Hongqiao (SHA) for domestic. Maglev train (¥50, 8 min) connects Pudong to Metro Line 2 — the coolest airport transfer in the world at 430 km/h.

💴

Currency & Budget

Chinese Yuan (RMB/CNY). ATMs (Bank of China, ICBC) accept foreign cards. Budget: ¥300–500/day for mid-range travel including food, transport, and entry fees.

🏨

Where to Stay

The Bund / Huangpu for luxury and views; French Concession for boutique and atmosphere; Jing'an for central convenience; Lujiazui (Pudong) for business travellers.

🎟️

Booking Ahead

Shanghai Museum, China Art Museum, and popular restaurants require advance reservations (WeChat Mini-Program). Book Disneyland at least 3 days in advance — it sells out on weekends.

Hidden Gems & Day Trips
Beyond the tourist trail
🌊
Day Trip

Zhujiajiao Water Town

A 30-minute drive from central Shanghai, this ancient water town features 36 stone bridges, Ming-Qing dynasty houses, and gondola rides along narrow canals. Far less touristy than Wuzhen but equally atmospheric — ideal for a half-day escape.

🚌 ~40 min from People's Square
🎡
Theme Park

Shanghai Disneyland

Asia's most modern Disney park opened in 2016, featuring the world's largest Disney castle (Enchanted Storybook Castle), a full-scale Pirates of the Caribbean ride, and uniquely Chinese-themed attractions. A full-day experience — arrive at opening to beat queues.

Metro Line 11 direct 💰 ¥400–750
🌱
Green Space

Botanical Garden & Chenshan

Shanghai Chenshan Botanical Garden, 45 km southwest of the city, spans 207 hectares with one of Asia's largest plant collections. Its sunken rock garden and mineral hot spring make it a relaxing contrast to urban Shanghai — especially beautiful during cherry blossom season.

Metro Line 9 → bus 💰 ¥50
