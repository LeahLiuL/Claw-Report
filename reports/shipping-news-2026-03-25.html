FMC Rejects Carrier Request to Waive 30-Day Waiting Period for Middle East Surcharges — CMA CGM, Hapag, Maersk, ZIM Must Wait Until April

The U.S. Federal Maritime Commission (FMC) has denied requests from CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, Maersk, and ZIM Integrated to waive the mandatory 30-day filing and waiting period before implementing new Middle East-related emergency surcharges. The carriers had argued that the rapidly evolving crisis warranted expedited implementation. The FMC's decision means these surcharges cannot take effect until early April at the earliest, providing temporary relief for U.S. importers and exporters.
Short-term positive for U.S. shippers: surcharge increases delayed by ~2 weeks. Medium-term: carriers will still impose fees in April; shippers should budget accordingly. FMC oversight continues to be a key constraint on rate actions in the U.S. trade.

08 Port Congestion

Houston Port Cuts Free Reefer Storage Time as Cold Chain Imports Outpace Overall Growth

The Port of Houston is reducing the free storage period for refrigerated import containers due to growing reefer dwell-time problems. Despite reefer volumes representing a small share of overall imports, their growth rate is significantly outpacing general cargo, creating long-dwell situations that consume limited reefer-plug infrastructure. Port officials have implemented the policy change to incentivize faster cargo pickup and free up reefer-capable ground slots.
Affects importers routing refrigerated cargo through Houston. Budget for additional storage costs if cargo cannot be picked up within the new (shortened) free time window. Consider pre-arranging inland transportation to avoid penalties.
