US DoJ Indicts 4 Asian Container Manufacturers for Price Fixing — CIMC, CXIC, Dong Fang, Singamas
美国司法部起诉4家亚洲集装箱制造商价格操纵 — 中集、新华昌、东方国际、胜狮
The US Department of Justice has indicted China's CIMC, CXIC Group Containers, Shanghai Universal Logistics Equipment (Dong Fang International Containers), and Hong Kong-listed Singamas Container Holdings for colluding to fix dry container prices from late 2019 to at least January 2024. Executives allegedly met at CIMC headquarters in Shenzhen on Nov 14, 2019, agreeing to restrict output and impose financial penalties on overproduction. Container prices roughly doubled from $1,600 to $3,500 during the pandemic. Singamas CEO Teo Siong Seng has taken leave; marketing director Vick Nam Hing arrested in France with US extradition pending.
美国司法部起诉中集（CIMC）、新华昌（CXIC）、上海环物流设备（东方国际集装箱）及香港上市胜狮集装箱控股，指控其从2019年末至至少2024年1月共谋操纵干箱价格。高管据称2019年11月14日在中集深圳总部会面，约定限制产量并对超产施加经济处罚。疫情期间集装箱价格从$1,600翻倍至$3,500。胜狮CEO张松声已请假，营销总监Vick Nam Hing在法国被捕，等待美国引渡。
