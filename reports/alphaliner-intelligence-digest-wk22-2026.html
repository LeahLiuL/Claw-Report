Iranian state TV confirmed on May 27 that a draft US–Iran framework commits Iran to restoring commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz to pre-war levels within one month, in exchange for a US military pullback from Iranian approaches. A 60-day ceasefire extension is included. Trump on May 23 had stated the broader agreement was "largely negotiated." The deal awaits Trump's final approval; however, per Al-Monitor and Gulf Today, Iran has agreed to continue managing shipping lanes, conducting inspections, and imposing service fees — the Hormuz corridor will not return fully to pre-February 28 conditions immediately.

COSCO SHIPPING Lines suspended all new bookings for routes to and from Middle East ports on May 19, citing continued Hormuz uncertainty and the need to reassess vessel positioning ahead of any corridor reopening. This affects JEA, Dammam, Oman, and other Gulf calls. The suspension is expected to be lifted following confirmation of the US–Iran draft framework. OOCL, as COSCO's affiliate, is expected to mirror this policy. This is the most significant capacity withdrawal from the Gulf since the February 28 crisis began.
